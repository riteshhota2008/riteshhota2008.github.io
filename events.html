Android CS Course 8th -13th April 2016

The Google Students Club organized a first of its kind "Applied CS with Android" course in SRM Kattankulathur from 8th - 13th April 2016. It was directly in collaboration with Google India and was focused on Android App Development.SRM was one of the few institutions in India selected to conduct the course.

The course saw an unprecedented turnout of more than 550 registrations, of which 26 participants were shortlisted after a series of screening tests, from all the three campuses, ie Ramapuram, Vadapalani and Kattankulathur. The workshop was facilitated by Muthu Ramakrishnan, who was the Android Facilitator for the course.

The course employed a unique Flip Classroom model, where students were enabled to learn concepts on their own and build an app a day based on their understanding of the concept. It was a 6 day, 6 unit course - equivalent to a 2 credit course in a university, upon completion of which the students were provided certificates.

The course saw students from different years implementing their skills and knowledge towards the given work. 4 students from first year, 21 from second year, 3 from third year and 1 student from fourth year took part in the course.

The course turned out to be a great success. Everyone involved were really happy with the knowledge they had gained during the duration of the course, not to mention the fact that the course enabled all the participating students to produce their own fully functioning app on more than one occasion.

Project Ara - Project Ara follows the modular phone building concept of the Ara phones. The attendees of the event will be first auctioned off different modules of a phone and then they have to go through a design sprint to design their phone. The last stage for the competition will be a Trade Fair, where the attendees will showcase their phones and also invest in other phones. The one with the most money at the end of the event will be the winner. The skills that the participants are tested on in this competition are Design thinking and how well they market their product.

Google Quiz - This event goes by the way of conventional quizzes, but with a twist. ALl the questions asked will be related to Google and its products. It will have 2 rounds, a general prelims and a team face-off.

GQuest - GQuest is a treasure hunt that follows the way through cryptic hints related to Google and all of its dealings. It will have an online preliminary round, followed by a round that will go on throughout the campus. The hints that the hunt will mainly deal with will be Android and Cloud related, along with some basic facts and figures.

Google Doodle - Drawing inspiration from Doodle 4 Google, this event will be a competition that judges the participants on how creative they are and also on how well they can market their product. The first round will be an on-site drawing competition where they will have to recreate the Google logo on the basis of a theme provided to them. The second round will be how well they market the art online. They can use social media and all other related tools to garner the maximum number of votes.

Design Infogram - This event takes designing to a different level. In this, the attendees have to design infographic posters related to certain Google products. They can use paper to go ahead with it, or even any other available tools, like Adobe Photoshop.

Photogrammatically Correct II - This is a photography contest that was very successful in last year’s version of Hangout. So, Hangout ‘15 will be reintroducing this competition in its second rendition. Participants will submit their work online, which will be shortlisted on the basis of votes. The second round will be judged by a professional photographer.

Android MOOC Jan-May 2015

The ‘Android Students Club’ initiative has started its chapter in SRM University and will help take android app development to the next level. This is a report for the first week of classes. This class was mainly an orientation for the course, giving the enrolled students a chance to understand what will be going on for the next months to come. It was conducted for 49 students who were shortlisted after a screening test (involving 113 applicants).

Overall objective of the event:

Week 1 aimed to be an introductory class where the students enrolled in the course could meet their facilitator and the GSA, be given an introduction to the courses they will be taking, network with their fellow students and understand basic Java and OOP concepts.

The ‘Android Students Club’ initiative started its chapter in SRM University and will help take android app development to the next level. This is a report for the second week of classes. This class introduced students to Android Studio and the basic layout of an app. It was conducted for 35 students.

Week 2 aimed to introduce Android Student to the students, take them through the basic layout of an app and help them build their first workable app and test it on their phones.

Week 3 aimed to teach the topics of- 1. Activities 2. How to switch between activities (intents) and 3. The android lifecycle and how all these play an important and basic factor in every Android application. The class ended with the students completing an app that switched between two activities, continuing the app the students made in the first week of classes.

Android Design Sprint | October 8, 2014

Android Design Sprint was an introductory session on Design Thinking (mainly focusing on Android Design). The purpose of the event was to introduce students to the whole process that goes behind Design thinking, give them a basic idea of what it is about and how it particularly relates to android. The end result was a prototype formed by all the teams. The event began with a session on design thinking and went on to a design sprint session where participants used the principles they learnt to solve sample use-cases and come up with a solution (in the form of a prototype). The top 3 teams will present their ideas at GDG Chennai’s DevFest, if they come up with a working prototype.

● 250 registrations; 60 students shortlisted for the event
● 10 prototypes formed at the end of the session

Time slots: 9:00- 11:30- Session on Design Thinking
11:30-12:00- Problem cases announced and teams formed
12:00-13:30- Break for lunch
13:30-15:30- Design Sprint
15:30- 16:30- Prototypes judged and best 10 asked to present
16:30- 17:00- Winners announced
17:00- 17:30- Certificates distributed to participants and registrations taken for Android Program

About Us

The team consists of 31 dedicated members who work hard to make the club successful. The Club is divided into 4 domains - Business, Creative, Public Relations and Technical.

The club has many future plans already in place. It plans to conduct another Applied CS course in the next academic year. Google Students Club conducts a week long fest 'Hangout' each year since its inception; which consists of various events and workshops. The workshops and events are conducted by the members of the club, with aid from authorized external personalities. Each year the fest witnesses a gigantic number of participation. We also conduct stand-alone workshops such as Design Sprint.

Google Students Club in SRM University aims to keep their good work intact while striving for further brilliance down the road.
